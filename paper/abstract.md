# Abstract

Vision-language-action models sample language-conditioned robot actions or trajectories from visual observations. score-tail selection stresses the scorer's upper tail: the selected action is not an average candidate, but the candidate ranked highest among many samples. We give an exact finite tie-aware selection law showing that high-N physical utility depends on selected-tail utility rather than average score-utility correlation. We define semantic affordance over-selection as the regime where selected language/vision plausibility improves with `N` while real physical utility saturates or collapses.

We introduce **Certified TailGuard** (`TailGuard` in code), an abstaining inference-time controller for finite VLA candidate pools. It first applies hard modeled-physics certificates for reach, swept collision, receptacle compatibility, stability, fragile/heavy handling, tool/object match, and blocked paths. It then calibrates selected-tail utility from pilot labels, applies empirical lower confidence bounds, compares against `N=1` and random baselines, and either selects a certified action, falls back to low-N certified selection, or abstains/request labels. Controlled simulator experiments, adversarial stress families, and component ablations show near-100% repair only when the modeled certificate covers the relevant failure modes. A guarded RoboCasa/LIBERO runner records external integration status, but no reset/step reward evidence or real-robot validation is claimed.
