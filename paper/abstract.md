# Abstract

Vision-language-action models sample language-conditioned robot actions or trajectories from visual observations. Best-of-N selection stresses the high semantic-score tail: the selected action is not an average candidate, but the candidate that a scorer ranks highest among many samples. We give an exact finite tie-aware selection law that characterizes selected real utility for a fixed generator/scorer stack. We define semantic affordance over-selection as the regime where selected language/vision plausibility improves with N while real physical utility saturates or drops. Controlled and learned VLA-style experiments show this failure through object-binding, reachability, collision, and receptacle errors. Physical grounding and pilot-label calibration repair selected-tail utility in toy settings. No real-robot validation is claimed.
